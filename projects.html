Full-stack genomic AI platform for antibiotic resistance prediction

GT-8 Calculator — 8-bit ALU on FPGA

Gate-level digital design and hardware verification in Verilog
Verilog HDLModelSim Quartus PrimeDE10-Lite FPGA

The GT-8 Calculator is an 8-bit arithmetic logic unit designed entirely in Verilog HDL on an Intel DE10-Lite FPGA. Rather than using behavioral shortcuts, the entire design was built from the gate level up — starting with individual logic gates and progressing through a full ripple carry adder to a complete ALU supporting six operations. This project was my first serious exercise in the kind of bottom-up hardware design thinking that VLSI work requires.

A key part of the project was writing a comprehensive self-checking testbench in ModelSim to validate correctness across all operations and edge cases, including signed overflow, maximum-value carry propagation, and two's complement subtraction.

Design Details
  • 8-bit ALU supporting ADD, SUB, AND, OR, XOR, and NOT with full carry, overflow, and zero flag outputs.
  • Built from gate level up through a complete ripple carry adder — no behavioral Verilog shortcuts.
  • Synthesized and deployed on the Intel DE10-Lite FPGA using Quartus Prime.
Verification
  • Self-checking ModelSim testbench with automated pass/fail reporting across all six operations.
  • Validated signed overflow, maximum-value carry propagation, and two's complement subtraction edge cases.
  • Achieved full functional verification before synthesis.
