Long-Term Focus

VLSI and Hardware Intelligence

The field I am most drawn to sits at the intersection of VLSI design and artificial intelligence. As neural networks grow more capable, the bottleneck is increasingly hardware: how do you implement inference efficiently on a chip with strict power and area budgets? That question requires both architectural depth and an understanding of data flow, which is exactly what my CHEA and DSSD thread preparation addresses.

Companies like NVIDIA, Apple, and a growing ecosystem of AI chip startups hire engineers who can reason from the dataflow of a transformer architecture down to the circuit-level tradeoffs of its implementation on silicon. My goal is to be that kind of engineer.
